mvp

We will develop an MVP for you from the ground up. MVP is short for a minimum viable product. It is a process of generating your first marketable product through a number of iterations. It helps businesses get customers’ feedback to understand what they need. An MVP is designed to minimize the waste of time and money on the projects that you will not be able to monetize successfully.

How an MVP can help your project:

  • Your product is developed and launched within the tightest deadlines. It means that anything you do not have time for will be postponed until future product iterations.
  • New versions and iterations are completed promptly, and your customers are always content with the product’s increasing quality.
  • Customer feedback allows you to concentrate on the desired features, making the product stand out from the competition.
  • Keeping a close eye on the customer feedback makes your MVP’s engagement levels rise which translates into higher sales.
  • Even if the MVP is not successful, the price of the business error is minimized. You can continue development by adjusting the product for other niches or rethinking the idea altogether.

We can provide the first version of an MVP in 6 to 8 weeks. We will employ only critical functionality and reduce the time to market to ensure each next MVP iteration is produced efficiently and promptly. Once you market the product, the first phases of user acceptance testing will define the further development approach.

prototype

We will develop your product prototype taking into consideration the project’s specifics, market research, and your potential customers’ requirements. The prototype is a way to visualize your project and test its feasibility before presenting it to the investors. The prototype allows you to demonstrate the future application and the business approaches you will take.

How a prototype can help your project

  • In a short span of time, you get a mock-up, a video or a presentation to use in your investment pitch. One demonstration is worth a thousand pages of detailed documentation.
  • You demonstrate the promised value for the potential customers and investors within a minimum timeframe.
  • You save money by avoiding the development of redundant products and testing their market need.
  • You can either use or discard the prototype during further development stages, as it is not designed for sale.

Your prototype will be in 2 to 3 weeks. We will design an interface and present the key features for you to eliminate the risk and save resources before you get the funding needed for an MVP.

Quality Assurance

Quality control is an integral part of software development process, aimed at fulfilling quality requirements. Testing is among the critical steps of the software quality control process, as it identifies flaws and bugs and helps eliminate them before the final release.

Our project testing process includes several steps:

  • Quality requirements' initial assessment. It allows QA specialists to prepare a tentative testing plan, complete with test specifications.
  • System analysis and the development of specifications. Later on, specifications can be used to create a testing plan.
  • Test cases development. You can specify the preferred system, like testlink, testrail, hiptest. We will employ test cases upon your request.
  • Testing automation. It ensures consistent testing results and continuous quality assurance of your project in the long run.
  • Iteration testing. With agile development, each iteration includes testing to fix problems without transferring them to the following iterations.
  • Final pre-release testing and verification. Testers evaluate the code, locate and fix bugs, polish the finished product.
  • Post-release support. Quality assurance tests are inescapable post-release to ensure immediate fixing of unforeseen issues.

Business Analysis

We will provide you with an analyst to investigate your business domain and work out an explicit System Requirements Specification (SRS). It is an outline of what you expect from your product. A good SRS serves as a reference for everyone involved in the project, from stakeholders to coders, designers, and testers. We will help you design a detailed SRS to minimize possible misunderstandings and delays at the development stage.

Your project will benefit from a good SRS:

  • You save time. When questions arise, you can refer all specialists working on the project to a single document that outlines your needs.
  • You save money. Ambiguous requirements waste your time, and you have to pay extra for the changes to the product, whatever engagement model you employ.
  • You communicate your needs to the development team more clearly, ensuring that specialists fully understand your needs and goals.
  • You feel confident. SRS provides a roadmap for your development team and decreases the risk of failure.

Code review / Consulting

Code review is an inherent part of the development process, as software products are created by human beings who are not invulnerable to mistakes. Agile software development utilizes code review to detect code flaws and bugs.

Code review possesses many benefits for your project:

  • You save money. Finding and fixing bugs is cheaper at the development stage than at QA.
  • You save time. The QA team’s workload is reduced if the review is performed correctly.
  • You get a well-documented code that matches the enterprise coding standards.
  • You achieve code maintainability by improving code organization and adding appropriate comments.
